RESEARCH FINDINGS (VR-Forces docs + MAK web + local files):
1. VR-Forces ground path planning uses generated NAVIGATION DATA (a nav mesh; Autodesk Gameware/
   Kynapse), per-terrain. The streaming "MAK Earth Space (online)" terrain PAGES IN on demand
   around objects (predictive), and "many objects over a wide area OR fast-moving objects" can
   defeat the predictive pager (vrf_terrainPaging.htm). You can force loading with a TERRAIN
   PAGE-IN AREA (a tactical-graphic object; back-end only; vrf_manuallyPageInTerrain.htm).
2. BLIND-MOVE FALLBACK (the C++ interface's escape): setNavigationEnabled(false) is a documented
   per-entity DataRequest that DISABLES use of navigation data -> direct/dead-reckoned move
   (vrf_setNavigationEnabled.htm). Unrealistic; break-glass only. Implementable in the facade.
3. Bogaland2 (the golden Sweden scenario, loaded EXPLICITLY for our Sweden runs) carries a
   "Blocking Terrain Page-In Area" over its region, on MAK Earth Space (online).mtf + the
   C2simEx.sms model set. COA-STP1/Mojave runs had NO page-in area -> HYPOTHESIS: the missing
   page-in area starves the Mojave terrain, so the leader path plans empty.
4. TERRAIN SURVEY: NTC (online).earth IS the Fort Irwin NTC terrain (local Razish berms
   NTCRazishBerm.tif, NTC buildings, NTC observer positions). Curated SoCal insets: 29Palms
   ~34.29/-116.15, Edwards AFB ~35.04/-118.13, plus Kilo2 (USMC camp) and Range 220. Only Kilo2
   + Range220 have PRE-GENERATED nav data (440/882 files) - but they are ~1m/1ft LAND-USE
   patches, not maneuver-scale. The COA-STP1 AO (34.68,-116.72) is ~65 km SOUTH of the NTC core,
   in GENERIC open Mojave, on NO curated/nav-data'd terrain.
5. AO IS FIXED: the COA is based on "Tropic Tortoise", a classic schoolhouse training scenario;
   its geography must stay. So the fix must make path planning work AT that generic spot (not
   relocate to Fort Irwin / a nav-data'd range).

ARTIFACT BUILT (programmatic scenario generation - a KEEPER capability):
6. TropicTortoise.scnx = a Bogaland2 variant. Bogaland2.scnx is a ZIP of pure-text S-expression
   parts; its .oob has only 3 objects (2 globals + the page-in area), no units. Generated by:
   relocating the page-in area's ECEF center + tait-bryan orientation from Sweden to the Mojave
   AO, keeping the ~60x122km body-vertices, renaming parts, repackaging. Coordinate math
   (ECEF<->geodetic; orientation yaw=lon-pi, pitch=lat-pi/2, roll=pi) was VERIFIED by exact
   round-trip against Bogaland's actual values (dX=dY=dZ=0.0000 m; dyaw=dpitch=0). Center
   34.615,-116.55. Loaded FIRST TRY (GUI confirmed: "Blocking Terrain Page-In Area 1" present,
   correctly placed over the units; terrain rendered).

CONCLUSION (page-in hypothesis FALSIFIED - clean):
9. The Terrain Page-In Area is NOT the aggregate-movement fix. Terrain paging is NOT the blocker
   (terrain is present + rendered; the area loaded and covers the units). Cross-check: R10 proved
   individual ENTITIES path-plan and march at Mojave (member vehicles 1.1-1.3 km, telemetry) - so
   the terrain surface is usable. The failure is SPECIFIC to the aggregate lead-follow LEADER-PATH
   planner, which emits junk at this generic-desert AO (empty -> freeze; bad goal -> wrong-way
   drift). This is a VR-Forces NAVIGATION-DATA / path-planner property of the location, NOT an
   interface defect and NOT terrain-surface. R9's "terrain content" verdict was directionally
   right (location-dependent) but the ACTIONABLE cause is nav-data, and "avoid the region" was
   wrong. (Supervisor note: the page-in idea was over-weighted vs R10; the A/B earned the
   correction - which is the point of running it.)
