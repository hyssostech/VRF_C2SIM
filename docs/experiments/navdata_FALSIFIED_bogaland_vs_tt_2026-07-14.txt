NAV-DATA HYPOTHESIS FALSIFIED - Bogaland vs TropicTortoise are the SAME terrain (2026-07-14)
============================================================================================
Trigger: user asked "Bogaland works - what's in there that is not yet in TT?" The comparison
falsifies the hypothesis that generated nav data (a NavMesh) is what makes Mojave aggregates move.

STRUCTURAL DIFF (unzipped both .scnx) - IDENTICAL except location + unit set:
  Bogaland2.scn AND TropicTortoise.scn both declare:
    (Terrain-Database     "$(SHARED_DATA_DIR)\...\MAK Earth Space (online).mtf")
    (Gui-Terrain-Database  same)
    (Simulation-Model-Set-Files "$(DATA_DIR)\simulationModelSets\C2simEx.sms")
  Both .oob contain a "Blocking Terrain Page-In Area 1" object. Neither references any nav area.
  Bogaland2's own scenario-name: "Scenario created on MAK Earth Space (online).mtf" - it is NOT a
  curated Bogaland terrain; it is the SAME whole-earth streaming terrain at Sweden coords.

NAV DATA COVERAGE (C:\MAK\SharedData\16\latest\TerrainData\navData):
  MAK Earth Space (online): ONE nav area only = "NavArea-ground-platform 1" at 34.5,-116.7 (MOJAVE,
  ours, generated 2026-07-14). The SWEDEN region (58.7,16.4) has NO nav data on this terrain.

THE FALSIFIER (evidence that was already in the repo):
  * semantic_units245_run1 (2026-07-14, SWEDEN, SubordinateFanOut OFF = genuine aggregate leader-
    path, NO fanout fallback, NO nav mesh): 14.MechBn 5318 m, 1222.MechPlt 5634 m, 1.BdeHQ 9016 m;
    317/552 objects moved >100 m. Aggregates march 5+ km with NO nav data.
  * R9_region_swap (2026-07-13): SAME code/day/settings/unit set, ONLY location differs.
      SWEDEN control: continuous motion; 45 member "Offset Route" creations.
      MOJAVE:        1.BdeHQ ~1.16 km then froze; 114.MechCoy ~410 m then froze; 1222.MechPlt ~8 m;
                     ZERO offset routes; vrfSim log: "moveAlong() - empty route -- not sending move
                     along to subordinate".
  Same terrain, same code, NEITHER region has nav data -> Sweden marches, Mojave freezes.
  Therefore nav data is NOT the cause of the Mojave aggregate freeze. The "aggregate leader-path
  requires a NavMesh" theory does not survive the Sweden control (Sweden aggregates path-plan with
  no NavMesh). That symptom was an unresolved falsifier the whole time; the nav-data thread was a
  detour from the R9 finding "Sweden has good leader-path planning, Mojave does not."

WHAT THE NAV-DATA WORK IS STILL GOOD FOR: nothing for THIS problem. The generation/loading mechanics
  we worked out (recipe, load-on-BE, the "(Disabled)" gotcha) are correct facts about VR-Forces but
  do NOT address the Mojave freeze. Ground-truth bonus from chasing it: vrfSim.log shows the BE loads
  the terrain + page-in area but never loads nav data on scenario open for this streaming terrain -
  a real fact, just not the fix.

THE REAL, RE-SCOPED CAUSE:
  Member OFFSET-ROUTE generation returns EMPTY at Mojave (0 routes) but succeeds at Sweden (45), on
  the SAME terrain/code. An aggregate moves by moving its members along formation-offset routes; at
  Mojave those are empty, so members - and thus the aggregate - do not move. Units DO receive real
  Mojave elevations (1131/1040/1116 m), so it is not void terrain. This is a region/terrain-specific
  route/offset-planning failure on the streaming terrain, NOT a nav-mesh gap. Root cause (terrain
  paging depth on the BE vs VR-TheWorld data at the Mojave AO vs an aggregate-movement setting) is
  the NEXT investigation - separate from, and better scoped than, nav data.

PROVEN MOJAVE MOVER (already built): SubordinateFanOut=true (R10) tasks member entities via STANDARD
  nav and moves them at Mojave, bypassing the empty-offset-route path. This is the pragmatic path to
  running coa-gpt scenarios at Mojave with visible movement. It is a different movement mechanism,
  not "avoiding the region".
